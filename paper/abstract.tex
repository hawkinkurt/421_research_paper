\begin{abstract}
This paper builds a gravity model of international film production flows and examines the effectiveness of national film production incentive schemes in attracting international film productions. I construct bilateral trade data using a dataset sourced from the Internet Movie Database (IMDb) covering the 600 top-grossing films per year from 1990 to 2023. I find that the gravity framework fits bilateral film production flows well, but the determinants differ in notable ways from those documented for goods trade. Cross-sectional estimates indicate that countries with active incentive schemes receive approximately $18\%$ more inward production, but this reduces to roughly $9-11\%$ under country-pair fixed effects. Early adopters (pre-2005) attract substantially more production than late adopters, while incentive effects show no evidence of compounding over time. 
\end{abstract}
